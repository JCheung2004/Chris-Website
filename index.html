Chris Badenoch's Place for Sounds

About

Hi I'm Chris Badenoch and this is my place for sounds on the internet. I'm a 23 year old music technology student at Oxford Brookes, looking to one day be able to sustain myself through music. Keen to learn and improve (by any means necessary) and hope I can share that process through this platform. I'm mainly a guitarist with a strong interest in electronic music and making things sound a bit weird.

My Gear


Epiphone Wildkat

Lovely hollow-body with a wide maple neck which was perfect for me. I learnt how to play on an old classical guitar and big, wide necks felt so much more comfortable. Humbucker pickups and the Bigsby tailpiece really appealed to me and I think this guitar has really shaped my playing style.

Yamaha FX370C

Electric-acoustic guitar I have used for pretty much everything.

Fender Squier Jazz Bass

Had some bassy needs so picked this up. Plays real nice but I'm not a very experienced bassist so can't comment on much else! I play it through the Exelsior since it has a 15" speaker or if I'm lazy (or it's late at night) I DI it.


Focusrite Scarlet 202i2 Interface

First piece of kit I picked up when I got frustrated with my 8-track. Really impoved workflow and while I've read negative reviews of these models I've never had any problems.

SM57

Robust & reliable dynamic mic which is usually what I fall back on when I'm not sure how to approach recording something. I use it mainly for isolated recordings of my amp or of percussion.

SE2200a II C

Not sure how necessary the full title is but regardless, I use this condenser for most recordings at home. Most often on acoustic guitar and vocal recordings, occasionally using it on the amp to pick up more of the room.


TC Electronic Ditto X2 Looper

Really long recording time, especially compared to the DL4, and with 0.5 speed & reverse effects. Whole days have slid by just messing around with this pedal, got pretty deep down the 'reversy guitar' rabbit hole and i'm still trying to climb out of it.

Electro-Harmonix Holy Grail

Really solid pedal; always a bit wary of digital reverb but this one's a beaut.

Line 6 DL4

Crazy amount of stuff you can do with this one. Love daisy-chaining it with the Ditto and the Holy Grail to build some atmospheric goodness.


Big Muff Germanium

Double distortion pedal, the options being 'distortion' or 'overdrive'. Use this pedal a lot to vent my bottomless rage, oh and to make good sounds.

Fender Exelsior Amp

Big, vintage lookin 13W amp with a 15" speaker. Has an amazing built in tremolo and I love it to bits but it weighs an absolute ton. Not for the feeble roadie I imagine.

M-Audio Axiom 49 MIDI Controller

Use this to program all software instruments. Nice drum pads and keys play well enough to accommodate my amateur piano abilities.
